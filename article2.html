Windsor FOG Bylaw: What You Need to Know

March 22, 2026 | By Vive Bio Solution

For restaurant owners and food service establishments in Windsor-Essex, understanding and complying with the local Fats, Oils, and Grease (FOG) Bylaw is not just good practice—it's a legal requirement. Non-compliance can lead to significant fines, operational disruptions, and environmental penalties. This guide will help you navigate the essentials of the Windsor FOG Bylaw and how Vive Bio Solution can assist in maintaining compliance.

What is the Windsor FOG Bylaw?

The Windsor FOG Bylaw is designed to prevent the discharge of FOG into the municipal sewer system. FOG accumulation in sewers causes blockages, leading to overflows, property damage, and increased maintenance costs for the city. The bylaw mandates that all food service establishments implement proper FOG management practices, primarily through the installation and regular maintenance of grease traps or interceptors.

Key Requirements for Windsor-Essex Businesses

  • Grease Trap Installation: All new and existing food service establishments are typically required to install an adequately sized grease trap or interceptor.
  • Regular Maintenance: Grease traps must be regularly inspected and cleaned to ensure they are functioning effectively. The frequency depends on the volume of FOG generated.
  • Proper Disposal: Accumulated FOG and waste from grease traps must be disposed of by licensed waste haulers.
  • Record Keeping: Businesses are often required to maintain records of grease trap maintenance, cleaning, and FOG disposal for inspection by municipal authorities.
  • Best Management Practices (BMPs): Implementing BMPs such as scraping food waste into bins before washing dishes, minimizing the use of hot water to flush FOG, and educating staff on FOG prevention are crucial.

Consequences of Non-Compliance

Failure to adhere to the Windsor FOG Bylaw can result in:

  • Significant Fines: Municipalities impose hefty fines for violations, which can quickly add up.
  • Sewer Backups: Blockages caused by FOG can lead to sewer backups, impacting your business and potentially neighbouring properties.
  • Operational Disruptions: Enforcement actions, including temporary closures, can severely disrupt your business.
  • Environmental Damage: FOG entering waterways can harm aquatic ecosystems.
