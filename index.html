How It Works

Not Chemistry That Moves Grease.
Biology That Destroys It.

Chemical drain cleaners push FOG downstream — it re-solidifies in your pipes or clogs Windsor's municipal sewer. Our bacteria convert FOG to CO₂ and water. It disappears permanently.

Lipase and protease enzymes activate on contact with FOG, breaking down the grease layer into microscopic droplets bacteria can attack.

The Biology Behind the Results
🦠
Bacillus subtilis

Our primary bacterial strain. A natural soil bacterium classified WHO Risk Group 1 — completely safe. Produces lipase, protease, and amylase enzymes to break down FOG.

⚗️
Enzyme System

Lipase breaks fat bonds. Protease handles proteins. Amylase tackles starch. Cellulase and keratinase address fibers and hair. 7 enzymes attacking every type of organic waste.

🔄
Beta-Oxidation

The metabolic pathway bacteria use to convert fatty acids into CO₂ and water. C₅₁H₉₈O₆ + O₂ → 51 CO₂ + 49 H₂O. Complete mineralization — no chemical byproducts.
